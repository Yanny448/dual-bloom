Philosophy

There are always two stories. The one we show. And the one we hide. My paintings exist between the two.

Selected works

Three canvases, three inversions. A warm body on a cold field; a cold body on a burning one; a machine wearing flesh-pink. Around each figure, white paint-marker symbols — drawn like tattoo flash — carry the second story. Stay long enough and the hidden hour appears in each one.

The story of the paintings

Symbols that reveal
themselves over time

I create contemporary symbolic paintings that explore the duality of the human experience.

Through bold compositions and symbolic imagery, I explore the tension between softness and sharpness, desire and restraint, dominance and vulnerability, beauty and obsession.

Every painting contains hidden visual references designed to reveal themselves over time, inviting you to return, look closer, and uncover new layers of meaning.

My work is an invitation for anyone who has ever felt too complex to fit into a single identity.

About me

Contradiction is one of the most honest parts of being human.

Painting became the place where they stopped fighting.

I've never fit comfortably into a single identity. Strength and sensitivity. Control and surrender. Logic and emotion. For years, these parts of myself felt like they were in constant conflict.

Every canvas allows opposing forces to exist together — to dance instead of compete. Hidden symbols become metaphors for the internal landscapes we rarely speak about.

I want each piece to feel different every time someone looks at it. The longer you spend with it, the more it reveals.
